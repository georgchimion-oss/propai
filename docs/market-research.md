## 3. User Pain Points (From G2/Capterra Reviews)

### What Property Managers Hate About Current Software
1. **Terrible support** — long wait times, limited availability, inconsistent quality
2. **Payment processing friction** — limited options, partial payment difficulties, poor banking integration
3. **Buggy interfaces** — emails not populating, texts not delivered, features breaking
4. **Overwhelming complexity** — simple tasks require too many clicks
5. **No proactive AI** — tools are reactive, not predictive
6. **Poor mobile experience** — managers are on-site, not at desks
7. **Disjointed systems** — maintenance in one tool, accounting in another, leasing in a third
8. **Vendor removed without notice** — trust erosion (Buildium/Vendoroo incident)

### What They Wish They Had
- One platform that does everything without being overwhelming
- AI that actually reduces their workload (not just a chatbot)
- Mobile-first experience for on-site management
- Instant tenant communication without manual follow-up
- Automated compliance tracking (especially post-SB 4D)
- Better vendor management and bid comparison

## 4. Florida Regulatory Opportunity

### SB 4-D: Florida Building Safety Act
Created after the Surfside condo collapse (Champlain Towers, June 2021). Creates massive compliance burden = software opportunity.

**Requirements:**
- Buildings 3+ stories: milestone structural inspections at 30 years (25 near coast), then every 10 years
- Phase 1: non-invasive visual review by licensed FL engineer/architect
- Phase 2: detailed testing if Phase 1 finds deterioration
- **Structural Integrity Reserve Studies (SIRS)** every 10 years
- Starting 2025: **associations CANNOT waive or underfund reserves** for structural elements
- Reports must be distributed to all owners, posted on websites, retained 15+ years
- **July 1, 2025**: All associations must create online account with FL Division of CTMH

**Non-compliance consequences:**
- Unsafe structure designation → potential evacuation
- Board member legal liability for fiduciary breach
- County fines for missed deadlines
- Insurance complications — non-compliant buildings can't get coverage
- Broward County: even stricter — repairs within 180 days of deficiency ID

**Software opportunity:**
- No formalized software mandated by statute — but 15+ year record retention, owner distribution, and online reporting create clear demand
- Compliance tracking, document management, reserve study integration, inspection scheduling — all automatable
- Board members personally at risk → strong motivation to buy compliance tools

## 5. AI Trends in PropTech (2026)

From industry analysis:

1. **Instant responses become standard** — AI answers every call, text, email immediately with contextual understanding
2. **Smart qualification shrinks the lead funnel** — automated prospect screening pre-qualifies before humans engage
3. **Viewings schedule themselves** — AI coordinates calendars, reduces no-shows by ~30%
4. **Compliance becomes continuous** — agentic systems handle right-to-rent checks, safety certs, license renewals
5. **Maintenance manages itself** — AI triages, classifies urgency, assigns contractors, tracks completion

Key shift: from **"AI as feature"** to **"AI as autonomous agent"** — AppFolio's Realm-X "Performers" are the clearest example.

## 6. UI/UX Design Intelligence

### What Top Property Management Software Looks Like
- **AppFolio**: Clean, modern, mobile-first. AI features embedded naturally into workflows
- **DoorLoop**: Modern UI, simple navigation, approachable for non-technical users
- **Buildium**: Functional but dated. Strong on financial views
- **Yardi**: Enterprise-complex, feature-dense, steep learning curve

### 2026 SaaS Dashboard Trends
- **Collapsible left-sidebar** — dominant navigation pattern (replaced top nav)
- **F-pattern layout** — North Star Metric in top-left quadrant
- **Dark mode** — standard for power-user tools
- **Action-oriented dashboards** — not static reports. Embedded tasks, "next best action" recommendations
- **Modular UI** — drag-and-drop widgets, global filtering, saved views
- **Data visualization restraint** — line charts for trends, bars for comparisons, donuts limited to 3 variables
- **Empty states with CTAs** — explain how to get data, not blank charts

### Design Principles for PropAI
- **Not another generic dashboard** — differentiate through AI-native interactions
- **Mobile-first** — property managers are on-site 60%+ of the time
- **Progressive disclosure** — simple surface, power underneath
- **Conversational UI for AI** — chat/command interfaces for AI agents, not just forms
- **Trust signals** — property management handles money, compliance, legal docs. UI must feel secure and professional
- **Tenant-facing ≠ Manager-facing** — two distinct experiences needed

### How PropTech Companies Acquire Customers
- Industry events: **CAI (Community Associations Institute)**, NARPM, NAA
- Local property management associations in Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach
- SFPMA (South Florida Property Management Association) — sfpma.com
- Direct outreach to condo boards (public records in FL)
- Free tier / freemium → conversion
- Referral programs with property management companies
- Content marketing (compliance guides, SB 4D explainers)

### Sales Cycle
- **SMB** (< 50 units): Self-serve, credit card, days
- **Mid-market** (50-500 units): Demo → trial → contract, weeks
- **Enterprise** (500+ units): Multiple stakeholders, RFP, months

## 8. Strategic Positioning for PropAI

### Gap in the Market
No one is doing **all of this** in one AI-native platform for South Florida condo associations:
1. AI maintenance triage with Florida-specific knowledge (hurricane, flood, structural)
2. SB 4-D compliance tracking and document management
3. Automated tenant/owner communication
4. Vendor RFP generation and bid comparison
5. Predictive maintenance
6. Tenant screening with Florida-specific regulations

### Why "Start in South Florida" is the Right Move
- Densest condo market in the US
- SB 4-D creates urgent compliance demand
- Property managers still using spreadsheets and email
- PwC research validates the opportunity (27/30 score)
- Local expertise = competitive moat vs national players
- Can expand to other FL markets, then nationally

### Competitive Advantages PropAI Could Have
- **Florida-first** — SB 4-D compliance baked in (no national player does this well)
- **AI-native from day one** — not bolted on like AppFolio/Buildium
- **Condo association focus** — most tools target rental properties, not HOA/COA
- **Six integrated AI modules** — not siloed point solutions
- **Built by someone who understands both AI and enterprise software** (Georg's background)
